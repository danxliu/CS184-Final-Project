Limitless: Infinity

Repulsive-surface optimization for self-intersection-free triangle meshes, implemented in C++/OpenMP with Barnes-Hut acceleration, Sobolev-style preconditioning, dynamic remeshing, and a genus-0-through-genus-5 canonical embedding gallery.

Team: Atharv Sampath Michael Pham Daniel Liu Elen Papyan
Proposal Milestone GitHub repo
01: Abstract

Repulsive Surfaces as the completed result

Our original proposal targeted Repulsive Shells, a SIGGRAPH 2024 framework for collision-free interpolation and extrapolation in shape space. During implementation we found that a paper-faithful graph-manifold shell solver was too large for the project window, so our final deliverable focuses on the underlying Repulsive Surfaces pipeline. We implemented tangent-point energy minimization on triangle meshes and reproduced the canonical embedding experiment for genus 0 through 5.

The completed system minimizes the discrete tangent-point potential from Repulsive Surfaces, accelerates far-field interactions with a BVH and block-cluster tree, computes Sobolev-preconditioned descent directions with GMRES, and remeshes dynamically to preserve triangle quality. The final gallery outputs converge from noisy or procedurally generated inputs to embedded low-energy surfaces.

Scope changed after the milestone: CUDA and the complete Repulsive Shells graph-manifold solver became stretch goals. We still implemented partial shell components, but the defensible final claim is the Repulsive Surfaces canonical-embedding reproduction.
02: Technical Approach

From exact TPE to a gallery optimizer

For a triangle mesh, we discretize tangent-point energy by midpoint quadrature over ordered pairs of distinct faces:

Phi_hat(x) = sum_{t1 != t2} a1 a2 |dot(n1, c1 - c2)|^6 / ||c1 - c2||^12

This potential grows when distant parts of the same surface approach intersection. Minimizing it therefore spreads a surface into an embedded low-energy shape without explicit continuous collision detection.

mesh -> face geometry -> TPE/BVH/BCT -> Hs-preconditioned direction -> line search -> remesh -> frame/energy logs
Geometry
We built mesh storage, OpenMesh conversion, normalization, procedural test meshes, and analytical face-geometry derivatives for centroids, normals, and areas. These derivatives are the basis for the TPE gradient, and every major gradient path was checked against central finite differences.
Energy
We first implemented an exact O(n^2) TPE energy and gradient as an exact baseline, then added a Barnes-Hut approximation using a BVH and block-cluster tree. A major correction was switching the accelerated kernel to projector cluster aggregates and an AABB distance MAC; this removed a large energy underestimation on remeshed torus geometry.
Descent
Plain L2 descent is strongly mesh-dependent, so we implemented a Sobolev-style preconditioned direction using cotangent Laplacians, a fractional high-order operator, and GMRES. During gallery descent we also remove global scale and barycenter modes.
Remeshing
Connectivity must adapt as the shape moves. Our remeshing pass performs edge splits/collapses, flips, and smoothing, while preserving the initial target edge length and enforcing a face-count budget. The final higher-genus runs stayed close to a 1.15x face-count ratio after 500 iterations.

Lessons learned

The largest failures were not syntax bugs; they were numerical modeling mistakes. A cluster aggregate that passed small tests gave a biased energy on irregular near-contact meshes. OpenMP summation order changed optimizer trajectories enough to produce visibly different surfaces. Remeshing changed the effective resolution target until we preserved the initial edge length. The practical lesson is that research graphics code needs exact baselines and strict regression tests around anything that changes the objective.

Genus 0 and 1 land on the expected round sphere and torus. The higher-genus examples are harder because the TPE landscape has many local minima; our shipped results are embedded, low-energy states rather than exact symmetry matches to every entry in the Repulsive Surfaces gallery.

Reference validation

The final meshes were checked against Repulsor's all-pairs TPE implementation. Representative brute-force parity ratios were 0.999999999999958 for genus 0, 1.000000000000016 for genus 1, 1.000000000000005 for genus 2, 1.000000000000108 for genus 3, 1.000000000000292 for genus 4, and 1.000000000000280 for genus 5. This validates the core TPE kernel independently of our own finite difference tests.

04: Limitations

What did not make the final claim

We made real progress toward Repulsive Shells, but not enough to call the complete graph-manifold interpolation or extrapolation pipeline reproduced. The elastic shell energy, adaptive TPE quadrature, path energy, and trust-region solver exist in the codebase; the remaining gaps are obstacle barriers, the production cloth-eversion and ball-through-tube examples, a block-diagonal Steihaug preconditioner, and the CUDA port.
